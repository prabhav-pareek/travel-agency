Trending Places in Uttarakhand

Jim Corbett

Jim Corbett National Park, located in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand, is India's oldest national park, established in 1936. Renamed in honor of the hunter-turned-conservationist Jim Corbett, the park is renowned for its rich biodiversity and as the launch site of Project Tiger in 1973. ​ Spanning over 520 square kilometers, the park features diverse landscapes, including hills, riverine belts, marshy depressions, and grasslands. It is home to a variety of wildlife, such as the Bengal tiger, Asian elephant, leopards, and over 580 species of birds. ​ Activities include jeep safaris, elephant rides, and bird watching. Nearby attractions like Corbett Falls and Garjiya Devi Temple add to the appeal.

Mussorrie

Perched at 2,000 meters (6,600 feet) in Uttarakhand, Mussoorie is a charming hill station renowned for its colonial architecture, panoramic Himalayan views, and vibrant cultural heritage. Located approximately 35 km from Dehradun, it offers attractions like the bustling Mall Road, serene Camel’s Back Road, and the scenic Kempty Falls. Whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or a romantic getaway, Mussoorie provides a perfect retreat amidst nature's beauty.

Kaichidham

Nestled in the serene Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, approximately 17 km from Nainital, Kaichi Dham is a revered ashram and Hanuman temple established in 1964 by the mystic saint Neem Karoli Baba. The name “Kaichi” translates to “scissors,” inspired by the unique confluence of two hills that form a scissor-like shape around the temple. Set at an altitude of 1,400 meters above sea level, the ashram is surrounded by lush forests and the tranquil Kosi River, offering a peaceful environment ideal for meditation and spiritual reflection. Kaichi Dham has attracted numerous visitors, including notable figures such as Steve Jobs and Mark Zuckerberg, who sought spiritual solace at this sacred site.
